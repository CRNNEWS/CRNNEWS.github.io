Accessibility & Closed Captioning

Our commitment to making news accessible to everyone

Accessibility Statement

CNN is committed to providing a website that is accessible to the widest possible audience, regardless of technology or ability. We strive to comply with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 at the AA level.

Features

  • Semantic HTML structure for screen reader compatibility
  • Keyboard-navigable interface for all interactive elements
  • Alt text on images for visual content description
  • Sufficient color contrast ratios throughout the site
  • Resizable text without loss of content or functionality
  • ARIA labels on interactive elements

Closed Captioning

CNN provides closed captioning on video content across our platforms. If you encounter a video without captions or experience issues with closed captioning, please let us know through our Feedback page.

Assistive Technology

Our website is designed to work with popular assistive technologies including screen readers such as JAWS, NVDA, and VoiceOver, as well as screen magnification tools and voice recognition software.

Ongoing Efforts

We continuously work to improve the accessibility of our site. Our team regularly audits our content and features to identify and address accessibility barriers.

Report an Issue

If you experience difficulty accessing any content on our site, please contact us through our Feedback page. We take all accessibility reports seriously and will work to resolve issues promptly.
